Realistic pace: at ~0.4kg/week, expect ~83.5kg by end of June (the last ~4 weeks of Phase 2A) and ~80kg by August. The old "81kg by June" target was never achievable from 84.5kg — and chasing it would mean an aggressive deficit that costs muscle. Slow and adherent wins.
✓
Plan the craving, don't fight it. One deliberate higher day/week (~2,300, high protein). It's a refeed, not a cheat — resets leptin, supports thyroid, kills the binge cycle. The "Monday weight jump" is water + glycogen, not fat.
!
Hunger guardrail: if "crazy hungry" is a daily pattern even after building meals for satiety, eat at the higher end (~1,950). A cut you can't sustain isn't a cut.
Why cut first instead of bulk? ＋
You already have 42kg skeletal muscle — "excellent" tier. The problem was never too little muscle; it's a fat layer hiding it. Bulking now adds muscle and fat on an already-covered frame = bulkier and softer. Cutting first reveals what's built. Your legs proved it.
Will the lost fat come back during the build? ＋
A little — and that's normal. You can't build muscle in a true deficit forever (the beginner/return recomp window closes). A controlled lean-gain adds ~1kg fat per 2–3kg muscle, then a mini-cut strips it. The floor keeps rising. Staircase, not loop.
Realistic timeline to abs + sharp face ＋
17–18% → ~12% ≈ 5–6kg fat at 0.4–0.5kg/wk = 12–16 weeks of a genuine deficit. Lower abs + jaw need ~11–12%. You're ahead on muscle & consistency, behind on ever running a real deficit. Lean, sharp, defined by year-end is realistic — if the cut starts now.

Why it won't move — three stacked causes
Dominant
Genetics
Mum has elevated cholesterol. Lp(a) 52.6 is almost purely genetic, diet-resistant — doesn't care about olive oil. The biggest mover isn't lifestyle-movable.
Improving
Hypothyroidism
Low thyroid lowers HDL + raises LDL via reduced LDL-receptor activity. LDL rising while TSH was 7.5 is textbook. TG crashing proves the gland is helping. Lipids lag TSH — tested too soon to see LDL benefit.
Controllable
Leanness + cardio
HDL responds to vigorous aerobic work + being leaner, not diet. Steady incline walk is moderate; you're at maintenance, not lean. Both suppress HDL.
